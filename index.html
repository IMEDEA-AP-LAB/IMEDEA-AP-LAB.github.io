Our research combines observational oceanography, numerical modeling, and data analysis to investigate physical processes in the Mediterranean Sea. ## Research Focus Our laboratory specializes in **Physical Oceanography** with particular emphasis on: - **Mediterranean Sea Dynamics**: Regional oceanographic processes and climate interactions - **Ocean Circulation**: Water mass formation, mixing processes, and circulation patterns - **Marine Sciences**: Interdisciplinary research combining physics, chemistry, and biology - **Coastal Oceanography**: Near-shore processes and land-sea interactions - **Climate Change**: Impact of global warming on Mediterranean oceanography ## Current Research Highlights - Investigation of Mediterranean water mass variability and deep water formation - Advanced numerical modeling of regional ocean circulation patterns - Climate change impacts on Mediterranean oceanography and marine ecosystems - Observational studies using autonomous gliders and oceanographic moorings - Air-sea interaction processes and their role in regional climate ## Facilities & Instrumentation Our laboratory is equipped with state-of-the-art instrumentation for oceanographic research: - **CTD sensors** for temperature, salinity, and depth measurements - **Autonomous underwater gliders** for long-term observations - **Oceanographic moorings** for time-series data collection - **High-performance computing** facilities for numerical modeling - **Satellite data processing** capabilities for remote sensing applications --- **We welcome collaborations** with international research institutions and are always looking for motivated students and researchers to join our team.
